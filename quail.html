  • Health Contents in Quail

    Quail’s are packed with vitamins and minerals. Even with their small size, their nutritional value is three to four times greater than chicken’s. Quails contain 13 percent proteins compared to 11 percent in chickens. Quails also contain 140 percent of vitamin B1 compared to 50 percent in chickens.

    In addition, quails provide five times as much iron and potassium. Unlike chickens, quails have not been known to cause allergies or diathesis. They help fight allergy symptoms due to the ovomucoid protein they contain. Regular consumption of quails helps fight against many diseases, strengthens the immune system, promotes memory health, increases brain activity, and stabilizes the nervous system. They help with anemia by increasing the level of hemoglobin in the body while removing toxins and heavy metals. The Chinese use quails to help treat tuberculosis, asthma, and even diabetes. If you suffer from kidney, liver, or gallbladder stones, quails can help prevent and remove these types of stones.
